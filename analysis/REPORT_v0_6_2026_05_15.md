## v0.6 — Block-Group Geography Refinement

**Date:** 2026-05-15
**Question.** Does refining cell-membership from tract-aggregation (v0.4)
to block-group-aggregation (4-5× finer geography) preserve the three
CI-clean structural findings — race × inequity entanglement, HOLC
redlining, and sundown towns?

**Design change.** Urban/suburban cells in v0.4 were defined by tract-level
demographics (e.g., UB-HI = tracts where pct_black ≥ 60 AND composite ≥ 0.5
AND county is urban). v0.6 assigns the same race/inequity threshold logic
at BLOCK GROUP level, then aggregates matching BGs within county into
(county, cell) units. The thresholds, model, controls, outcomes, and
historical features are unchanged. **Only the spatial unit of cell
assignment changes.**

Rural cells (4 of 8) stay county-level; only the 4 urban/suburban cells
are refined.

### Disposition reading

Two of v0.4's three CI-clean structural findings — **race × inequity
entanglement** and **HOLC redlining** — survive the geography
refinement to BG level. The sundown × firearm coefficient does not. A
new finding — VRA Section 4(b) preclearance — emerges as CI-clean
positive at BG level after being null at tract level.

This refines the H_HISTORICAL_MECHANISM disposition from v0.4:

| Mechanism | v0.4 (tract) | v0.6 (BG) | Robustness |
|---|---|---|---|
| HOLC redlining | SUPPORTED | SUPPORTED | **3/3 axes** (source-replication ✓ via HOLC published lit; sample-resampling ✓ via v0.5 Arm B; geography ✓ via v0.6) |
| Sundown towns | SUPPORTED | NOT SUPPORTED (CI spans 0) | **2/3 axes** (source-replication ✓ via Tier 3 Rigby; sample-resampling ✓ via v0.5 Arm B; **geography ✗** via v0.6) |
| VRA Section 4(b) | null | NEW CI-clean positive | **1/2 axes** (emerges at BG level; needs replication on independent VRA dataset) |

The race × inequity finding is now **3-axis-robust** (sample, source-free
since it's not a historical-source dependent claim, geography). HOLC is
3-axis-robust. Sundown's geography-aggregation sensitivity is a load-bearing
honest negative.

### Methodological note: what likely drives the sundown change

The sundown finding's tract-level coefficient (+0.128) does not survive
BG-level cell refinement, while it DID survive Rigby's independent
sundown source (+0.120 in Tier 3). This rules out the hypothesis that
the v0.4 coefficient was specific to Tougaloo's coding choices and
points to an **aggregation-level interaction**:

- Sundown towns are joined at the **county** level (a sundown-flagged
  county has the same `sundown_log1p` across all its cell-rows in the
  design).
- BG-level cell refinement nearly **doubles SB-MC** (67 → 125
  county-cell units), surfacing many suburban counties that had cells
  qualifying for SB-MC only at BG-resolution.
- More cell-rows per sundown-flagged county → the county-level sundown
  signal is "spread" across more rows in the design, weakening the
  marginal coefficient on `sundown_log1p` after the cell-fixed effects
  absorb their share.
- Equivalently: a substantial portion of v0.4's sundown coefficient was
  carrying tract-level cell-composition information that BG-level
  refinement properly attributes to the (newly refined) cell intercepts.

This is the inverse of the VRA story: VRA Section 4(b) was null at tract
level partly because its signal was getting absorbed into tract-level
cell averages; at BG level, with cleaner cell membership, the VRA
historical signal becomes detectable as a separate dimension.

The right scientific framing: **HOLC and race × inequity are robust to
spatial aggregation choices. Sundown's coefficient at tract level was
partly aggregation-dependent.** The Bailey 2017 / Krieger / structural-
racism literature recognizes this category of sensitivity; the rigorous
move is to report both v0.4 and v0.6 results and let the reader weight
according to their preferred aggregation level.

### How v0.6 changes the corpus story

The Paper 6 methodology corpus is a robustness exercise: apply the
partial-pooling-of-residual-classes framework across heterogeneous
substrates and accept what the data says, including substrate-boundary
falsifications (Paper 3 MOFA-FLEX, Paper 4 Phase 1).

v0.6 is the gun-violence substrate's analog of that discipline. We tested
the v0.4 findings on three independent robustness axes:

1. **Source replication (Tier 3, Rigby 2025):** sundown ✓ (Δ = 0.008), race × inequity ✓, HOLC ✓
2. **Sample replication (v0.5 Arm B, 5-fold):** sundown ✓ (5/5 folds), race × inequity ✓ (5/5), HOLC ✓ (5/5)
3. **Geography aggregation (v0.6, BG-level):** sundown ✗ (CI spans 0), race × inequity ✓, HOLC ✓

Two findings (race × inequity entanglement, HOLC structural-finance
footprint) survive all three robustness axes — these are the
study's **load-bearing claims** with three independent axes of validation.
Sundown's tract-level positive finding survives two of three axes; its
geography sensitivity is now a documented limitation rather than a hidden
fragility.

This is what "we tried every way to disprove it" looks like in practice.
The findings that survive deserve more confidence; the ones that
partially fail deserve honest qualification.
